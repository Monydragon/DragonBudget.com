How to audit subscriptions

A subscription audit means listing every recurring service, checking renewal dates and billing cycles, then deciding what to keep, pause, cancel, or monitor.

Start with the list

Write down the service name, cost, renewal date, billing cycle, account, and cancellation link. Include annual subscriptions because they can surprise a budget.

Find review candidates

Look for unused services, duplicate tools, price increases, and subscriptions due before payday. Dragon Budget’s planned Subscription Watch feature should summarize these candidates.

Simple example

Three small services at $10, $15, and $20 per month cost $540 per year. Canceling one can change safe-to-spend more than expected.
