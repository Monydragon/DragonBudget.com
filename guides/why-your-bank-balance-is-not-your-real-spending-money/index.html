Guide

Your bank balance is not your real spending money

A balance tells you what is in the account now. It does not automatically tell you what is already promised to bills, plans, and goals.

Your bank balance is not your real spending money because some of that money may already be needed for upcoming bills, subscriptions, groceries, savings, or a buffer. Safe-to-spend is a clearer number for purchase decisions.

What the balance misses

A bank app can show $900 while rent, a phone bill, groceries, and transportation are all coming due. The balance is true, but it is incomplete for decision-making.

A simple example

If your balance is $900 and $650 is already needed before payday, the useful spending question starts around the remaining $250, not the full $900.

How to make it clearer

Write down what is due before the next paycheck. Subtract planned expenses, goals, and a buffer. Dragon Budget turns that list into a safe-to-spend estimate and lets you test one purchase.
